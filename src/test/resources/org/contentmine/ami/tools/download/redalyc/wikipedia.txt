<h2 id="mp-tfa-h2" style="margin:0.5em; background:#cef2e0; font-family:inherit; font-size:120%; font-weight:bold; border:1px solid #a3bfb1; color:#000; padding:0.2em 0.4em;"><span id="From_today.27s_featured_article"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="From_today's_featured_article">From today's featured article</span></h2>
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:0.1em 0.6em;"><div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Neferefre.jpg" class="image" title="Statue of Neferefre in the Cairo Egyptian Museum"><img alt="Statue of Neferefre in the Cairo Egyptian Museum" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Neferefre.jpg/120px-Neferefre.jpg" decoding="async" width="120" height="160" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Neferefre.jpg/180px-Neferefre.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/30/Neferefre.jpg/240px-Neferefre.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="667"></a>
</div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Neferefre" title="Neferefre">Neferefre</a></b> was a <a href="/wiki/Pharaoh" title="Pharaoh">pharaoh</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Fifth_Dynasty_of_Egypt" title="Fifth Dynasty of Egypt">Fifth Dynasty</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ancient_Egypt" title="Ancient Egypt">ancient Egypt</a> in the 25th century BC, during the <a href="/wiki/Old_Kingdom_of_Egypt" title="Old Kingdom of Egypt">Old Kingdom</a> period. Known as prince Ranefer before he ascended the throne, he was probably the eldest son of pharaoh <a href="/wiki/Neferirkare_Kakai" title="Neferirkare Kakai">Neferirkare Kakai</a> and queen <a href="/wiki/Khentkaus_II" title="Khentkaus II">Khentkaus&nbsp;II</a>. Neferefre started <a href="/wiki/Pyramid_of_Neferefre" title="Pyramid of Neferefre">a pyramid for himself</a> in the royal necropolis of <a href="/wiki/Abusir" title="Abusir">Abusir</a> and was buried in it, although it was unfinished.  The pyramid was roughly completed by his brother <a href="/wiki/Nyuserre_Ini" title="Nyuserre Ini">Nyuserre Ini</a>, who succeeded him after the rule of <a href="/wiki/Shepseskare" title="Shepseskare">Shepseskare</a>. Fragments of Neferefre's mummy indicate that he died in his early twenties, after two years of reign.  A single text shows that he had planned or just started to build a sun temple called <i>Hotep-Re</i>, meaning "<a href="/wiki/Ra" title="Ra">Ra</a> is content" or "Ra's offering table". Like other Fifth Dynasty kings, he benefited from a <a href="/wiki/Funerary_cult" title="Funerary cult">funerary cult</a>, but the cult was abandoned by the late Old Kingdom. Because his pyramid and mortuary complex were unfinished, they may have attracted less attention from grave and stone robbers than nearby pyramids.  (<b><a href="/wiki/Neferefre" title="Neferefre">Full&nbsp;article...</a></b>)
</p>

<p><b><a href="/wiki/Bosc%27s_fringe-toed_lizard" title="Bosc's fringe-toed lizard">Bosc's fringe-toed lizard</a></b> (<i>Acanthodactylus boskianus</i>) is a medium-sized species of lizard found in northern Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. Active during the day, they are energetic foragers for insects and other small invertebrates, and are one of the most common lizards in their range. Males and females are similar in appearance, both having a snout-to-vent length of between 5 and 8&nbsp;cm (2.0 and 3.1&nbsp;in), but males are usually larger. The feet have long slender digits that are fringed. The <a href="/wiki/Anatomical_terms_of_location#Dorsal_and_ventral" title="Anatomical terms of location">dorsal</a> surface is olive-grey with five longitudinal dark stripes, the middle one of which subdivides at the neck, while the <a href="/wiki/Anatomical_terms_of_location#Dorsal_and_ventral" title="Anatomical terms of location">ventral</a> surface is whitish, but in the female, the underside of the tail becomes suffused with red during the breeding season. In juveniles, the tail is blue.
</p><p>This picture shows two <i>A.&nbsp;b.&nbsp;asper</i> lizards photographed in <a href="/wiki/Dana_Biosphere_Reserve" title="Dana Biosphere Reserve">Dana Biosphere Reserve</a>, Jordan, engaging in a love bite, a courtship ritual that may be connected to certain chemical cues present in the skin.
</p>
