Abstract

Five species of the grass genus

Spartina
are invading salt marshes along the Pacific coast of North America, of which three have been documented in British Columbia, Canada, in only the last decade. A taxonomic synopsis of the two native (
Spartina gracilis
,
Spartina pectinata
) and five introduced
Spartina
 taxa (
Spartina anglica
,
Spartina alterniflora
,
Spartina densiflora
,
Spartina patens
,
Spartina ×townsendii
) in the Pacific Northwest is presented to facilitate their identification, including nomenclature, a new taxonomic key, new descriptions for a subset of taxa, and representative specimens.
Spartina ×townsendii
 is newly reported for the flora of British Columbia. The non-coastal species
Spartina pectinata
 is reported from an urban site in British Columbia, the first confirmed report of the taxon for the province. Lectotypes are newly designated for
Spartina anglica
 C.E. Hubb.,
Spartina maritima
 subvar.  fallax St.-Yves, and
Spartina cynosuroides
 f. major St.-Yves.
